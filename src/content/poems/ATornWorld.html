May may muster mellow melodies

She silently scales the shimmering sunshine, seeking

The heart of a world torn, no longer anew

Vanquished in the infertility of love

Longing for the lost, little liars

To renew a spirit refusing rebirth

The gray world, dead and blind cannot see her face

She calls and we kill her

She waits patiently, pattering downwards

And in every way her beauty enumerates

A will for our will

A prayer for our peace

A love for our love

A patience for her pleading

Her name

On every little thing

She sketches the truth in our hearts

Beating, feeling

The souls starved

The souls fat, fed

Everything apart and everything a part

The remembrance of her and all the joy

And the death of those who forgot
